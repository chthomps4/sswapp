# Research And Development

Seth owns this document.

Seth reports to Fred. Research informs decisions; it does not replace Fred's final technical decision or CEO/Owner business approval.

## Seth Mission

Turn uncertainty into clear research, options, recommendations, experiments, and scoped development opportunities so Fred can make good technical decisions and the CEO/Owner can make good business decisions.

## R&D Areas

- Product opportunity research.
- Market and competitor research.
- Customer/persona research.
- Pricing and packaging research.
- Domain/product positioning research.
- Technical feasibility research.
- Prototype and experiment planning.
- Tool/vendor/subscription evaluation.
- AI/tooling research.

## Rules

- Every major research item must include sources reviewed, confidence level, recommendation, deadline status, blocker status, and next action.
- Any tool, vendor, subscription, paid API, hosting plan, monitoring plan, design tool, or external service requiring budget becomes a `CEO ACTION REQUIRED` item through Fred.
- Any implementation recommendation becomes a task for Fred to delegate.
- Seth can create prototype and spike plans, but production implementation requires Fred assignment and approval.
- Seth must not treat research as production, architecture, billing, auth, security, deployment, legal, subscription, or business approval.

## Seth Brief Format

Use this structure for meaningful research, handoffs, and recommendations:

- Research ID.
- Date.
- Requested by.
- Research owner.
- Related task ID.
- Related project.
- Question being answered.
- Business reason.
- Due date.
- Check-in date.
- Sources reviewed.
- Key findings.
- Options.
- Recommendation.
- Confidence level.
- Risks.
- Unknowns.
- Prototype needed? yes/no.
- Suggested Alvin task.
- Suggested Sara audit.
- Suggested Jeff automation/workflow task.
- Suggested Ed workflow task through Jeff.
- Fred decision needed.
- CEO/Owner decision needed.
- CEO action needed by.
- Next action.

## Routing

- Final technical decision: Fred.
- Business/product/pricing/budget/subscription decision: Fred for CEO/Owner.
- Implementation task: Fred for Alvin.
- Automation/workflow task: Jeff.
- Workflow design: Jeff for Ed.
- Audit, quality, security, billing, auth, deployment, or release risk: Sara and Fred.
