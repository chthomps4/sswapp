# Sample Workshop: AI Workflow Setup For Businesses

Status: internal draft for Fred review
Owner route: Jeff owns workflow, Ed drafts through Jeff, Seth researches market fit, Sara audits risk, Fred approves.

## Workshop Promise

Show a business how to turn scattered work into a review-safe AI-assisted workflow without giving AI final approval over publishing, money, security, or production changes.

## Target Audience

- Local service businesses.
- Small teams with scattered requests across chat, email, documents, and spreadsheets.
- Founders who need better visibility without adding a full operations department.

## 60-Minute Draft Flow

| Time | Segment | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0-10 min | Current workflow map | Identify where requests, approvals, files, and follow-ups currently live |
| 10-20 min | Tool stack fit | Decide what chat, tracker, docs, code repo, and deployment tools already exist |
| 20-35 min | Safe AI lanes | Pick draft-only tasks AI can help with and decisions humans must keep |
| 35-45 min | Command intake | Build a `!task` style intake template and routing rules |
| 45-55 min | Review gates | Define who approves content, code, spending, client data, and public claims |
| 55-60 min | Next step | Pick one manual-first pilot workflow |

## Workshop Safety Notes

- Do not use private client data in live AI examples.
- Do not connect paid tools during the workshop unless pre-approved.
- Do not imply AI replaces legal, security, billing, HR, or executive approval.
- Keep outputs as draft recommendations until reviewed.
